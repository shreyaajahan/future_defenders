Child Labour

Raising awareness and fighting for children's rights.

What is Child Labour?

Child labour refers to the exploitation of children through work that deprives them of their childhood, potential, and dignity. It is a serious issue that affects millions of children worldwide, forcing them into hazardous environments instead of allowing them to enjoy a safe and fulfilling childhood.

Why Address Child Labour?

  • Protects children from exploitation and harm.
  • Ensures access to education and development opportunities.
  • Contributes to breaking the cycle of poverty.
  • Promotes safer and fairer societies.

Laws Against Child Labour

Many countries have enacted laws to protect children from being forced into labor. For example, the Child Labour (Prohibition and Regulation) Act in India restricts children under 14 years from working in hazardous industries. Similarly, international organizations like the United Nations and ILO advocate for the eradication of child labor globally.
